WHAT THE COLORS MEAN

Dark / Black — Low error level → Original, unmodified pixels. These regions are clean.
Yellow / Orange — Medium error → Possible compression artifacts. Could be benign or minor edits.
Bright Red / White — High error level → Likely tampered region. Pasted, cloned, or edited after original save.

💡 ELA works by re-saving the image at a known compression level and measuring pixel differences. Edited regions get re-compressed from a "perfect" state, appearing brighter than surrounding untouched areas.

ELA (Error Level Analysis) detects tampering by measuring how differently image regions compress. Red/bright areas indicate potential forgery — they were edited after the original save.
